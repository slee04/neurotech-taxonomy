#### The current definitions ontology
  The published definitions implicitly stack three filters, not one:
  1. Neural specificity — targets the nervous system
  2. Activity/function — records or modulates (or augments) dynamic neural processes
  3. Device/technology — engineered artifact, not a drug or surgical procedure

Brain vs neurvous systems - differing definitions 

#### Problems/shortcomings of the current definition ontology 
* I want each component of the definition to have TIPSS+ justification / relevance. But the current definitions' #2 and #3 feel somewhat arbitrary. For example, the definitions often exclude structural brain imaging, because they implicitly carry a temporal dimension. But from a TIPSS perspective, this boundary is somewhat arbitrary and a technical convention, not based on an ethical dimension. 
* If we drop this arbitrary temporal domain, the definition can expand quickly.
    * Retinal OCT -> images CNS
    * Brain biopsy -> samples CNS
    * Neuropharamachology -> modulates CNS and neural activity chemically 
    * Neuropsychological tests -> infers cognitive function 
* This is also an interesting problem 3C's-wise, because for example modulation can easily converge/combine with static imaging, or with neuropharmachology.
* I want each component of the definition to have TIPSS+ implications / relevance 
* Why is neurotech special? From all the things listed below as what can be monitored/modulated by neurotech, is the level of control agency/autonomy/sovereignty/rights to decide to really fundamental parts of who we are/crossing the boundaries. This I think is also the reason why things like exercise or aromatherpay is not of concern whereas BCI is. A close analogy would be being handed a marketing flyer on the street vs. having Google monitoring all your online activities and showing ads on your personal device that targets exactly based on who you are and your personal history.

#### Mitigation 
* Claude's suggestion: follow the convention for the definition, but flag the TIPSS boundary
  mismatch. Something like: "The taxonomy follows consensus definitions focused on neural activity. However, TIPSS concerns extend to any technology that generates neural-specific data — including structural imaging and neural proxies. These are included as borderline technologies because their TIPSS profiles overlap significantly with core neurotechnologies, even though they fall outside strict definitions."
